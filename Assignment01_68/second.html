Red-Eared Slider

The Red-Eared Slider is a semi-aquatic turtle and the most popular pet turtle in the US. These turtles get their name from the small red stripe around their ears. They spend most of their time in water, and feed on plants and small aquatic creatures. Indoor housing should include UV lighting for warmth, a source of water, and space to bask.

African Side Neck

The African Side Neck turtle is very unique - they cannot retract their heads completely into their shells like most turtles. These turtles require a large amount of water to swim in, and don't do well in cold temperatues. For food, they love eating lettuce, small carrots, and other vegetation.

9 mins

Eastern Box Turtle

Eastern Box Turtles are characterized by their high-domed brown shell, covered with red markings. They are a little picky when it comes to home environments, prefering humid air for good respiration, warm temperatures, and a large body of water. While they are a bit shy, any tasty treat will bring them out of their shell. A healthy eastern box turtle can live up to 100 years, so be prepared to care for this pet for a long time!

Western Painted Turtle

The Western Painted Turtle features one of the most uniquely designed shells of all turtles. On top, they have a flat, brown shell, but the bottom of their shells reveals beautiful red marks, like a painting. As pets, these turtles need a filtered water tank and light to bask in. They are shy, often preferring to remain in their shells, but once they get used to their new family, they will make themselves visble.

9 mins

Mississippi Map Turtle

The Mississippi Map Turtle is small compared to their peers, females growing up to 10 inches and males only up to 5 inches. They generally live up to 30 years old, a short life for a turtle. They are timid creatures so a peaceful environment would be the best living space for these little creatures. As omnivores, they love eating fresh, leafy vegetables and greens. Since these turtles tend to get stressed easily and are generally more diffucult to care for, they would be best for experienced turtle keepers.

9 mins

Spotted Turtle

Spotted Turtles are well-suited to live as pets. This species is particulary distinctive due to the yellow spotted markings they develop on their upper shell area.They love to be active during the day, and rest at night. These turtles are not good swimmers, so water levels must be kept low. A temperature between 75-80 degrees is ideal for these pets. As for food, they are primarily carnivorous and love eating shrimps, worms, crickets, and other insects.
